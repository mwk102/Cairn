# The Cairn Manifesto
## What Cairn Believes

Cairn believes that meaningful places are worth remembering.

Maybe it's a quiet campsite at the end of a forgotten forest service road. Maybe it's the overlook where you watched the sunset with your family. Maybe it's the river bend where you caught your first trout.

These places become part of our lives.

A Cairn is more than a coordinate on a map. It's a place connected to memories, experiences, and stories.

## Why Cairn Exists

For years, I've saved my favorite places in Google Maps or tried to remember where they were.

Eventually the map becomes cluttered with hundreds of random pins, and I forget why I saved them in the first place.

Cairn exists because remembering why a place mattered is just as important as remembering where it is.

## Who It's For

Cairn is for people who love exploring.

People who find incredible campsites, fishing spots, trailheads, overlooks, waterfalls, and hidden places they want to return to.

It's for families building traditions.

It's for photographers chasing beautiful light.

It's for overlanders discovering forgotten roads.

It's for anyone who has ever said:

"I wish I could remember exactly where that place was."

## What Cairn Will Never Become

Cairn will never become another social network.

There are no followers.

No likes.

No algorithms.

No endless feeds.

No pressure to share every place you visit.

Cairn is a personal place journal.

Sharing is intentional and always your choice.

Your places belong to you.

## Our Philosophy

Discover.

Remember.

Return.

Every Cairn tells a story.

## Northstar

Google Maps helps you find places. Cairn helps you remember why they matter.
